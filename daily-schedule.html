11:00 - 12:00

Non-vanishing of L-functions at the central critical point

The values of L-functions at the central critical point s = 1/2 are of crucial importance in number theory. Perhaps the most important example is critical value of the L-function L(s;E) attached to an elliptic curve E, where the order of vanishing of L(s,E) at s = 1/2 is conjectured to be the rank of the curve, by the Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture. In general, it is believed that the vanishing of L-functions at s = 1/2 should be explained by arithmetic reasons, as for elliptic curves. For L-functions attached to Dirichlet characters, there is no such arithmetic reason, and a famous conjecture of Chowla predicts that those L-functions do not vanish at s = 1/2. While Chowla’s conjecture is still open, we will explain some recent progress towards the conjecture. The talk will be expository and accessible to undergraduate students.

Certains concepts ou objets mathématiques ont été pressentis intuitivement très longtemps avant qu’ils reçoivent des définitions mathématiques rigoureuses. Les raisons pour de si longues genèses sont diverses : parfois le concept n’avait pas d’utilité mathématique apparente, parfois les mathématiciens s’entêtaient à croire que l’objet était impossible. Polynomial Approximation in Dirichlet and de Branges-Roynyak Spaces

We may naively say the mathematical analysis is nothing but the approximation methods. This claim is not an absolute exaggeration. As a matter of facts, in many results of analysis we can easily observe that, either directly or indirectly, the approximation themes show their faces. In this talk, after some preliminaries, we study the approximation of analytic functions by polynomials. As a remedy for the failure of classical Taylor polynomials, various summations methods, e.g., Cesaro and Abel summations, are also discussed.
